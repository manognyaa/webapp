Early Life and Background

Shreya Ghoshal was born on March 12, 1984, in Berhampore, West Bengal, India. Her passion for music was evident from a young age, and she began her classical training in music at the age of four. Ghoshal's remarkable talent was recognized early on, and she participated in various singing competitions during her school years.

Ghoshal's breakthrough came with the song "Bairi Piya" from the film "Devdas" (2002), composed by Ismail Darbar. The song showcased her versatility and range as a singer, earning her widespread acclaim. Since then, Ghoshal has delivered numerous chart-topping hits, collaborating with renowned composers such as A.R. Rahman, Pritam, and Vishal-Shekhar. Some of her notable collaborations include "Teri Ore" from "Singh Is Kinng," "Barso Re" from "Guru," and "Deewani Mastani" from "Bajirao Mastani.
